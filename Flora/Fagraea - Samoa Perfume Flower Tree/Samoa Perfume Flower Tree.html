DescriptionSamoa Perfume Flower Tree is a large shrub to small tree with thick leathery leaves, about 10-19 x 4.5-10 cm, tip rounded, base narrowed. Leaf-stalks of the apical pair of leaves joined and covering the branch-end bud. Midrib is raised on the upper surface, leaf-stalks flat or convex on the upper surface. Flowers are white, turning to yellow, pleasantly perfumed, narrowly funnel-shaped, tube usually 7-10 cm long. Calyx is about 10-15 mm long. Fruits are ellipsoid to globular, about 3-5.5 x 2.5-4.5 cm. Calyx is persistent at the base. Samoa Perfume Flower Tree is native to Samoa Islands.
