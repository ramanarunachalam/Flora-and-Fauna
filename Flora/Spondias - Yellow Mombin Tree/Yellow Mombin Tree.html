DescriptionA small deciduous tree up to 20 m (60 ft.) high and 1.5 m (5 ft.) in girth, moderately buttressed; bark thick, corky, deeply fissured, slash pale pink, darkening rapidly, branches low, branchlets glabrous; leaves pinnate, leaflets 5-8 opposite pairs with a terminal leaflet, 10 X 5 cm (4 X 2 in.), oblong or oblong lanceolate, broadly acuminate, glabrous; flowers (Jan.-May) sweet-scented, in large, lax terminal panicles of small white flowers; fruits (July-Sept.) nearly 4 cm (1.5 in.) long, ovoid yellow, acid, wrinkled when dry; 1 seed. The fruits have a sharp, somewhat acid taste and are edible.
