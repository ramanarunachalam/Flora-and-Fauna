DescriptionPhilippine Tung is a small deciduous tree with a dense crown. Leaves are glossy, heart-shaped, and have an abnormally long stalk. Flowers are borne in drooping branched clusters at the end of branches. Flowers are white, flushed with pink, and have 5-7 oblong petals which are turned backwards. When the flowers appear, the tree is nearly leafless. Fruit is nearly spherical, densely velvety. The fruit is said to be poisonous to humans.
