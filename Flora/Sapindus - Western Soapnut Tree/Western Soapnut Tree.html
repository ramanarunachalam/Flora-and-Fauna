DescriptionSoapberry is an attractive evergreen tree with a dense, broad, globose crown; usually growing 9 - 15 metres tall with occasional specimens larger. The short bole can be 56cm in diameter. The fruit is a rich source of saponins and is often used as a soap - it is sold in local markets. The tree also supplies an oil, insecticide, timber and seeds that are used as beads. It is sometimes grown for its fruit and is often planted as an ornamental and to provide shade in the tropics and subtropics
